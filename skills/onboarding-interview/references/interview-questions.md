# Interview Question Bank

Per-file questions for the onboarding interview. **Ask one at a time, wait for the answer, push
for specifics, never pre-fill.** Skip questions already answered (read the file and earlier answers
first). Each file maps to `~/work-profile/<file>`. When you have enough, draft the file in place
using its existing headings, then run the reaction pass (see `SKILL.md`).

The **core three** (`identity`, `role-and-responsibilities`, `current-projects`) are the default
onboarding. The other seven are filled on demand or when a domain skill first needs them.

---

## identity.md — read-first, keep it short
1. What's your name and current role or title?
2. What organization or team are you with, if any?
3. If you explained what you actually *do* to a smart stranger at dinner — not your title — what
   would you say?
4. What do people come to you for? The "you should talk to ___ about that" thing.
*Enough after 3–4. A few facts + one paragraph. Don't pad.*

## role-and-responsibilities.md — the operational reality
1. Walk me through a typical week — what recurs without fail?
2. What are you directly accountable for — where it's on you if it doesn't happen?
3. What routine decisions do you make (the weekly ones, not the big strategic calls)?
4. What do you actually produce — manuscripts, grants, code, analyses, datasets, slides, reviews?
5. Who do you report to? Who reports to you, if anyone?
6. Any monthly/quarterly rhythms that shape your work — planning, reviews, study sections, board?
*Enough after 4–6. Capture the operational reality, not every edge case.*

## current-projects.md — active workstreams + their funding
1. What are you actively working on right now? Name them out.
2. [Per project] Tell me about it: what it is, where it stands, and what "done" looks like.
3. [Per project] Who are you working with on it?
4. [Per project] How is it funded — grant/contract, agency, internal, unfunded — and what's the
   funding status (active, under review, in prep, renewal due)?
5. Rank them by priority right now. Anything stalled or blocked?
*Cover each project the user names — don't force a number.*

## team-and-relationships.md — professional only, NO PHI
1. Who are the 5–8 people you interact with most? Names and roles.
2. [Per person] What's the relationship, and how do you interact — meetings, async, project-based?
3. [Per person] What do they need from you, and you from them?
4. [Per person] Anything an agent acting for you should know — style, sensitivities, context?
*Reuse collaborators already named under projects rather than re-asking.*

## tools-and-systems.md — a practical inventory
1. What tools and platforms do you use every day? Your core stack.
2. How is your setup customized — configs, integrations, workflows worth knowing?
3. Where does your important data live — repos, drives, databases, platforms? (Paths, not keys.)
4. Anything you're evaluating or about to adopt?
5. Anything you tried and deliberately dropped, and why?
*Enough after 4–5.*

## communication-style.md — precision matters most here
1. Generally brief and to the point, or more context and detail?
2. How formal is your work writing? Does it shift by audience?
3. What makes you think "that doesn't sound like me" when something's written for you?
4. Words, phrases, or patterns people would recognize as your voice?
5. Words/phrases you actively avoid — things that sound fake or not-you?
6. How do you structure an email — lead with the ask, background first, bullets, prose?
*Enough after 5–6. Push hard if answers are vague.*

## goals-and-priorities.md
1. What are you trying to accomplish in the next few months — goals, not the project list?
2. Longer term — this year, the next couple? What are you building toward?
3. On tradeoffs — speed vs. quality, short- vs. long-term — where do you generally land?
4. What are you explicitly NOT prioritizing right now, even though it matters?
5. If the next six months go well, what's different?
*Enough after 4–5.*

## preferences-and-constraints.md — clear rules, not a personality profile
1. Hard constraints on your time/availability an agent should know — time zone, off-limits hours/days?
2. Your non-negotiables — how work gets done, formatted, or delivered?
3. What do you hate? Where's your reaction strong — meeting types, jargon, output formats?
4. Personal constraints affecting work — travel, schedule, health — only what you're comfortable sharing?
5. When an AI produces something for you, your formatting preferences — length, structure, tone?
*Enough after 4–5. The no-PHI/no-secrets rule is already in the file; confirm it, don't relitigate.*

## domain-knowledge.md
1. Your areas of genuine expertise — what you could teach?
2. The jargon of your world a general AI might over-explain or get wrong?
3. Field context an outsider wouldn't know but that shapes everything — regulations, funding/review
   dynamics, instruments, data types?
4. Frameworks or mental models you use to think through problems?
5. Flip side — where are you a beginner and want MORE explanation, not less?
*Enough after 4–5.*

## decision-log.md — the examples are the point
1. How do you generally make decisions — analyze, gut, talk it out, sleep on it?
2. What information do you want before you call it?
3. Tell me about a significant decision you made recently — what was it, how did you think it through?
4. Another, ideally a different kind of decision?
5. How do you handle deciding without enough information?
6. Any decision you're currently sitting with?
*Push for specifics on at least two real decisions.*
