# Identity

**Name:** Maya Okonkwo
**Role / title:** Associate Professor of Biomedical Engineering; Director, Wearable Sensing Lab
**Organization / team:** Riverside University School of Medicine (joint appointment in Neurology)

## What I do
I build wearable sensors that measure things clinicians currently can't track at home — gait,
tremor, autonomic signals — and I run the studies that prove whether the measurements actually mean
something. Half my time is engineering (firmware, signal processing, the analysis pipeline) and
half is translational research: designing validation studies, getting them through the IRB,
recruiting participants, and turning the data into papers and grant renewals. I run a lab of six
(two postdocs, three PhD students, an engineer) and collaborate with two clinical departments.

## What I'm known for
Bridging the gap between a sensor that works on the bench and one that produces a clinically
trustworthy number. People come to me when they have a signal they can measure but can't yet
defend — I'm good at designing the study that turns a measurement into evidence.
