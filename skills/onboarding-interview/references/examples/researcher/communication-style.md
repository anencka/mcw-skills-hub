# Communication Style

## Overall
Concise and direct. I lead with the point and keep context to what's needed. Warmer with trainees,
crisper with program officers and reviewers.

## Writing tendencies
Short paragraphs, active voice, concrete nouns. I use precise technical terms but explain them when
writing for a clinical audience. I dislike hedging stacks ("it may potentially be possible that").

## Formatting preferences
Email: the ask in the first sentence, then 2–3 lines of why, then a clear next step. Bullets for
anything with more than three parts. For docs, a one-line summary up top before the detail.

## What I dislike
AI-flavored phrasing — "delve", "in today's fast-paced world", "it's important to note",
"furthermore". Em-dash-heavy throat-clearing. Anything that sounds like a press release.

## By context
- **Trainees:** encouraging but specific; I name the fix, not just the problem.
- **Clinical collaborators:** plain language, lead with the clinical implication.
- **Reviewers / program officers:** formal, evidence-first, no overclaiming.

## Words I use / avoid
- Use: "clinically meaningful", "defensible", "what does done look like", "ground truth".
- Avoid: "leverage", "synergy", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary".
