# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of expertise
Wearable biosensor design (firmware to enclosure), biomedical signal processing, clinical
validation study design, and the regulatory/IRB path for investigational devices.

## Jargon I use without definitions
SNR, drift, baseline wander, ICC and Bland–Altman (agreement stats), MDC (minimal detectable
change), sham/placebo arms, IDE, predicate device, sensitivity/specificity. Don't over-explain
these.

## Field context an outsider would miss
A sensor "working" in the lab says nothing about clinical trustworthiness — agreement vs. a
reference standard and test–retest reliability are what matter. Study budgets and timelines are
gated by IRB cycles and recruitment, not engineering. NIH paylines and the resubmission cycle drive
much of the lab's calendar.

## Frameworks / mental models I use
"What does done look like?" before starting anything. Measurement first: define the reference
standard and the agreement target before collecting data. Separate "can we measure it" (feasibility)
from "does the number mean something" (validation).

## Where I'm a beginner (explain MORE here)
Health-economics / reimbursement strategy, and formal FDA submission writing — explain these
thoroughly, I don't live in them.
